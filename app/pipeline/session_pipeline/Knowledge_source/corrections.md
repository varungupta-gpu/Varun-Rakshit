# Global Analysis

## Inefficient Front-Foot Weight Transfer

### Definition

The batter attempts to play predominantly on the front foot but fails to transfer body weight efficiently onto the lead leg, reducing stability and limiting effective force transfer during stroke execution.

### Identification Criteria

- Front Foot Progression remains high while Weighted Center of Mass shows limited forward movement.
- Front Knee Loading remains inconsistent throughout Preparation and Downswing.
- Front Foot Progression is not supported by corresponding body mass transfer.
- The body remains supported on the back leg despite forward foot movement.

### Correction Cue

Move your body with your front foot rather than simply reaching forward. Let your weight follow the stride.

### Impact

- Improves balance during attacking strokes.
- Allows more efficient transfer of momentum.
- Produces better timing and cleaner contact.
- Reduces reaching away from the body.

---

## Excessive Back-Foot Dependence

### Definition

The batter remains excessively supported on the back leg even when the movement pattern requires controlled forward transfer.

### Identification Criteria

- Back Foot Progression dominates throughout Preparation.
- Weighted Center of Mass remains biased towards the rear leg.
- Front Knee Loading remains minimal.
- Front Foot Progression is limited across the session.

### Correction Cue

Allow the hips and body to travel forward with the stride instead of remaining over the back leg.

### Impact

- Improves attacking intent.
- Creates smoother weight transfer.
- Enhances front-foot stroke execution.
- Improves movement efficiency.

---

## Inconsistent Lower-Body Strategy

### Definition

The batter does not demonstrate a repeatable lower-body movement strategy across the session, alternating between front-foot and back-foot loading without clear intent.

### Identification Criteria

- High variability in Front Foot Progression.
- High variability in Back Foot Progression.
- Weighted Center of Mass shows inconsistent movement direction.
- Large statistical variation across multiple deliveries.

### Correction Cue

Develop a repeatable loading pattern before adapting to specific deliveries.

### Impact

- Improves repeatability.
- Creates a stronger technical base.
- Improves movement consistency.
- Simplifies shot preparation.

# Stance

## Head Outside Base

### Definition

The batter begins the stroke with the head positioned outside the base of support, reducing balance before movement initiation.

### Identification Criteria

- Head Stability indicates consistent lateral displacement during Stance.
- Weighted Center of Mass begins outside the support base.
- Trunk Lateral Flexion indicates excessive lean.
- Hip–Shoulder Alignment suggests compensatory upper-body positioning.

### Correction Cue

Keep the nose centred between the feet and maintain level eyes before initiating movement.

### Impact

- Improves balance.
- Creates a stable visual reference.
- Improves repeatability.
- Supports efficient movement in later phases.

---

## Excessively Wide Stance

### Definition

The batter adopts a base that is wider than necessary, restricting efficient lower-body movement.

### Identification Criteria

- Stride Width remains consistently above the normal operating range.
- Lower-body movement during Preparation is restricted.
- Weighted Center of Mass demonstrates limited mobility.

### Correction Cue

Bring the feet slightly closer together while maintaining an athletic base.

### Impact

- Improves mobility.
- Allows smoother weight transfer.
- Enhances adaptability.
- Improves rotational freedom.

---

## Excessively Narrow Stance

### Definition

The batter stands with insufficient base width, reducing stability before movement begins.

### Identification Criteria

- Stride Width remains consistently narrow.
- Head Stability demonstrates increased variability.
- Weighted Center of Mass exhibits excessive sway.

### Correction Cue

Increase the distance between the feet enough to create a stable athletic position.

### Impact

- Improves stability.
- Enhances balance.
- Provides a stronger movement foundation.
- Reduces unnecessary body sway.

---

## Closed Initial Body Alignment

### Definition

The batter begins with excessive body closure, restricting efficient movement toward the off side.

### Identification Criteria

- Hip Direction indicates persistent closed orientation.
- Shoulder Line Progression Angle remains closed.
- Stride Line Progression Angle supports reduced openness.

### Correction Cue

Relax the front shoulder slightly and allow the body to face the bowler more naturally.

### Impact

- Improves movement freedom.
- Enhances off-side stroke access.
- Supports smoother rotation.

---

## Excessively Open Initial Alignment

### Definition

The batter begins with excessive openness, exposing the body too early before movement begins.

### Identification Criteria

- Hip Direction consistently indicates open orientation.
- Shoulder Line Progression Angle remains excessively open.
- Hip–Shoulder Alignment demonstrates early rotational bias.

### Correction Cue

Maintain a neutral athletic position without exposing the chest too early.

### Impact

- Improves stability.
- Delays unnecessary rotation.
- Creates a stronger technical base.

---

## Poor Initial Weight Distribution

### Identification Criteria

- Weighted Center of Mass remains biased toward one leg.
- Head Stability indicates compensatory movement.
- Preparation begins with inconsistent loading.

### Impact

- Improves readiness.
- Supports efficient trigger movement.
- Improves repeatability.

---

# Preparation

## Excessive Forward Press

### Definition

The batter commits excessively onto the front foot during Preparation, reducing adaptability to different deliveries.

### Identification Criteria

- Front Foot Progression becomes excessive.
- Weighted Center of Mass shifts rapidly forward.
- Front Knee Loading dominates before Downswing.

### Correction Cue

Move forward with control rather than lunging toward the ball.

### Impact

- Improves balance.
- Maintains shot options.
- Reduces over-commitment.

---

## Insufficient Forward Press

### Definition

The batter demonstrates limited forward movement during Preparation, reducing effective weight transfer.

### Identification Criteria

- Front Foot Progression remains minimal.
- Weighted Center of Mass remains static.
- Front Knee Loading remains limited.

### Correction Cue

Allow the front foot to initiate controlled movement toward the ball.

### Impact

- Improves body loading.
- Supports front-foot stroke execution.
- Creates smoother momentum transfer.

---

## Excessive Back-and-Across Movement

### Definition

The batter moves excessively onto the back foot during Preparation, delaying forward movement.

### Identification Criteria

- Back Foot Progression dominates.
- Weighted Center of Mass remains rear biased.
- Front Foot Progression remains limited.

### Correction Cue

Use the back foot to load rather than retreat.

### Impact

- Improves attacking options.
- Creates balanced loading.

### Impact

- Creates repeatable movement.

### Impact

- Improves bat path.

## Delayed Hip Loading

### Definition

The batter delays loading the hips during the Preparation phase, resulting in reduced body organization before initiating the downswing.

### Identification Criteria

- Hip Direction changes very late during Preparation.
- Lower-body loading remains incomplete until the transition into Downswing.
- Hip–Shoulder Alignment remains static despite trigger movement.

### Correction Cue

Begin loading the hips naturally as the trigger starts rather than waiting until the bat begins moving.

### Impact

- Improves movement sequencing.
- Produces smoother transitions.
- Creates a stronger technical foundation.

### Identification Criteria

- Upper Body Rotation lacks consistency.

### Correction Cue

Allow the shoulders to remain relaxed and organized while the lower body completes loading.

### Impact

- Improves movement consistency.
- Supports efficient bat swing.
- Enhances overall body organization.

## Excessive Trigger Movement

### Definition

The batter performs an unnecessarily large trigger movement that introduces excessive body displacement before the stroke begins.

### Identification Criteria

- Large changes in Front Foot Progression.
- Significant COM displacement.
- High Head Stability variability during Preparation.

### Correction Cue

Reduce the size of the trigger and move only enough to prepare the body.

### Impact

- Improves timing.
- Improves repeatability.
- Reduces unnecessary movement.

# Downswing

## Early Hip Opening

### Definition

The hips rotate before efficient lower-body loading has been completed, reducing rotational sequencing.

### Identification Criteria

- Hip Direction changes rapidly before efficient weight transfer.
- Hip–Shoulder Alignment decreases prematurely.
- Hip rotation consistently begins before trunk organization.

### Correction Cue

Allow the body to complete loading before aggressively rotating the hips.

### Impact

- Improves sequencing.
- Improves timing.
- Enhances power generation.

---

## Early Shoulder Opening

### Definition

The shoulders rotate ahead of the lower body, reducing efficient bat delivery.

### Identification Criteria

- Shoulder Line Progression Angle changes early.
- Upper Body Rotation begins before efficient hip contribution.
- Hip–Shoulder Alignment decreases rapidly.

### Correction Cue

Keep the front shoulder closed slightly longer and allow the hips to lead.

### Impact

- Improves bat path.
- Improves shot control.
- Creates smoother rotation.

---

- Large statistical variation in rotational features.

### Correction Cue

### Impact

- Improves rotational efficiency.
- Improves timing.
- Produces smoother stroke mechanics.

# Follow Through

## Cut Follow Through

### Definition

The batter stops the bat prematurely after contact.

### Identification Criteria

- Follow Through length remains consistently short.
- Body rotation ends early.
- Limited extension across deliveries.

### Correction Cue

Finish every stroke completely and allow the bat to travel naturally.

### Impact

- Improves rhythm.
- Improves balance.
- Improves repeatability.

---

## Poor Recovery Balance

### Definition

The batter struggles to regain balance after stroke completion.

### Identification Criteria

- COM remains unstable.
- Head Stability decreases after execution.
- Excessive lateral movement continues.

### Correction Cue

Finish in a balanced position with the body under control.

### Impact

- Improves readiness.
- Improves consistency.
- Reduces unnecessary movement.

### Correction Cue

### Impact

- Improves balance.

# Head Position

## Early Head Lift

### Definition

The batter lifts the head before completing the stroke.

### Identification Criteria

- Head Stability decreases near contact.
- Neck position changes rapidly.
- Body posture rises early.

### Correction Cue

Watch the contact area slightly longer before lifting the head.

### Impact

- Improves visual tracking.
- Improves contact quality.
- Improves consistency.

---

## Head Outside Base Throughout Stroke

### Definition

The head remains consistently outside the body's support base throughout the movement.

### Identification Criteria

- Persistent Head Stability deviation.
- COM remains displaced.
- Trunk Lateral Flexion remains elevated.

### Correction Cue

Maintain the head over the body's base throughout the complete stroke.

### Impact

- Improves balance.
- Improves timing.
- Creates a repeatable batting action.

---

## Excessive Head Movement

### Definition

The batter demonstrates unnecessary head movement throughout multiple phases of the batting action.

### Identification Criteria

- High Head Stability variability.
- COM fluctuations mirror head movement.
- Large statistical variation across deliveries.

### Correction Cue

Allow the body to move underneath a stable head rather than moving the head with the body.

### Impact

- Improves visual stability.
- Improves shot consistency.
- Supports repeatable technique.

---

## Static Head Restricting Movement

### Definition

The batter keeps the head excessively rigid, restricting natural body organization.

### Identification Criteria

- Minimal head movement despite substantial body movement.
- Reduced COM progression.
- Limited trunk contribution.

### Correction Cue

Maintain head stability without becoming rigid; allow natural body movement beneath the head.

### Impact

- Improves movement freedom.
- Improves body coordination.
- Supports efficient stroke mechanics.

# General Coaching Principles

- Prioritize movement efficiency over stylistic appearance.
- Reinforce strengths before correcting weaknesses.
- Corrections should target recurring patterns across multiple deliveries rather than isolated actions.
- Every correction must be directly supported by biomechanical evidence.
- Distinguish between a player's natural batting style and technical inefficiencies.
- Recommend only practical, evidence-based coaching interventions.
- Focus on improving repeatability, balance, sequencing, and efficient force transfer rather than changing an effective individual technique.
