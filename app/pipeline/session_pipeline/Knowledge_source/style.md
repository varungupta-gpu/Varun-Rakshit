# Batter Base Analysis

## Overview

The Batter Base Analysis is designed to evaluate the batter's natural setup before stroke execution. Unlike shot-specific analysis, this assessment focuses only on the **Stance** and the **early portion of the Preparation phase** across multiple deliveries.

The objective is to identify the batter's default movement tendencies, technical habits, and repeatability before the bat begins accelerating toward the ball.

Since the analysis is performed over multiple deliveries, the emphasis is placed on recurring movement patterns rather than isolated observations from a single shot.

The resulting analysis should describe the batter's natural batting base, movement organization, and consistency.

---

# Scope of Analysis

Only analyze the following portions of the batting action:

- Entire Stance Phase
- First Half of the Preparation Phase

Ignore:

- Late Preparation
- Downswing
- Follow-Through
- Shot Outcome
- Ball Direction
- Bat Path
- Shot Type

The objective is **not** to evaluate stroke execution, but to understand how the batter naturally prepares before playing the ball.

---

# Inputs

The following information will be provided:

1. Biomechanics JSON
2. Feature Statistics JSON
3. Biomechanical Feature Documentation
4. Batting Style Analysis Documentation

Use the documentation to correctly interpret the biomechanical features and their significance.

---

# Objective

Analyze the batter's natural batting base across all deliveries.

The analysis should identify recurring movement characteristics including:

- Initial body alignment
- Lower-body organization
- Upper-body organization
- Head position
- Body balance
- Weight distribution
- Trigger movement
- Initial weight transfer
- Rotational preparation
- Stability
- Movement consistency

The focus should always be on identifying long-term technical habits rather than delivery-specific variations.

---

# Analysis Strategy

Evaluate all provided deliveries collectively.

Do not treat every delivery independently.

Instead:

- Identify recurring movement patterns.
- Ignore occasional variations unless they occur consistently.
- Give greater importance to movements that appear across the majority of deliveries.
- Describe the batter's natural setup rather than exceptional movements.

---

# Areas to Analyze

## 1. Initial Stance

Describe:

- Open, Neutral or Closed stance
- Hip organization
- Shoulder organization
- Foot alignment
- Base width
- Overall body posture

Focus on how the batter naturally positions the body before initiating movement.

---

## 2. Trigger Movement

Describe how the batter initiates movement.

Examples include:

- Minimal trigger
- Forward press
- Back-and-across trigger
- Static setup
- Small weight shift
- Pronounced trigger movement

Discuss:

- Magnitude
- Direction
- Consistency

---

## 3. Initial Weight Transfer

Describe how body weight begins shifting during the first half of preparation.

Focus on:

- Front-foot loading
- Back-foot loading
- Center of mass movement
- Lower-body support

Do not classify the eventual shot type.

Only describe the initial loading strategy.

---

## 4. Body Organization

Evaluate:

- Hip alignment
- Shoulder alignment
- Hip–Shoulder coordination
- Trunk posture
- Upper-body organization
- Lower-body organization

Determine whether the batter maintains an efficient and repeatable body organization before the downswing.

---

## 5. Head Position

Describe:

- Head stability
- Head alignment
- Consistency
- Balance

Head position should be evaluated throughout the stance and early preparation only.

---

## 6. Repeatability

Since multiple deliveries are provided, evaluate movement repeatability.

Discuss:

- Consistency of stance
- Consistency of trigger
- Consistency of weight transfer
- Consistency of body organization
- Delivery-to-delivery variation

This section should summarize how repeatable the batter's base is.

---

# Coaching Perspective

The analysis should resemble the observations made by an experienced batting coach watching the batter before the ball is played.

Focus on questions such as:

- Does the batter have a repeatable setup?
- Is the base balanced?
- Is the trigger controlled?
- Is movement efficient?
- Is the batter stable before committing to the stroke?
- Does the batter appear technically organized?

Avoid discussing shot execution.

---

# Confidence

Every major observation should include an appropriate confidence score.

Confidence should depend upon:

- Number of deliveries supporting the observation.
- Consistency across deliveries.
- Agreement between multiple biomechanical features.
- Stability of the movement pattern.

Reduce confidence when:

- Deliveries show inconsistent behaviour.
- Features contradict each other.
- Large movement variability exists.

Confidence should reflect the repeatability of the movement pattern rather than the certainty of the language model.

---

# Output

Generate a structured coaching report describing the batter's natural batting base.

The report should include:

- Overall Base Summary
- Stance Analysis
- Trigger Movement Analysis
- Initial Weight Transfer
- Body Organization
- Head Position
- Repeatability Assessment
- Overall Technical Impression

The final report should describe the batter's default setup and movement identity before stroke execution rather than analyzing individual deliveries or shot outcomes.
